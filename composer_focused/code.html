Can you explain how quantum entanglement works in simple terms?
smart_toy Framr Core

Quantum entanglement is a physical phenomenon that occurs when a pair or group of particles is generated, interact, or share spatial proximity in a way such that the quantum state of each particle cannot be described independently of the state of the others.

Imagine two magic coins. When you flip one and it lands on heads, the other coin instantly lands on tails, no matter how far apart they are. Einstein famously called this "spooky action at a distance."
